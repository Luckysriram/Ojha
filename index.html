Layer 1 — Foundation

Ojha CPR + Supertrend

The base system. CPR classifies the day type, Pivot levels set targets, Supertrend confirms trend direction. Every other layer is a filter on top of this foundation.

Central Pivot Range
TC / Pivot / BC
Day-type classifier. Narrow CPR = trending day expected. Wide CPR = choppy day. Calculated from previous day's High, Low, Close.
Pivot Levels
S1 S2 S3 / R1 R2 R3
Targets once in a trade. R1/S1 = first target. R2/S2 = trail on strong momentum. R3/S3 = rare extreme trend days only.
Supertrend
Trend confirmation
Green = bullish bias. Red = bearish. Never trade a CPR breakout when Supertrend conflicts with the direction.

CPR width interpretation

Narrow CPR — trending day
Previous day had a small range. Expect a breakout above TC or below BC with follow-through. The system's primary opportunity.
Wide CPR (400–600 pts on Nifty) — choppy day
Both sides of CPR will get tested. Avoid directional buying. Prefer option-selling strategies.
Overlapping CPR — continuation bias
Today's CPR overlaps yesterday's. Today's CPR above yesterday's = bullish carry. Below = bearish carry.

Layer 1 — Filters

When to Stay Out

The system's explicit no-trade rules. These save more money than the entries make.

Wide CPR — choppy market
Previous day large range (400–600 pts Nifty). Entries stopped out repeatedly. Only trade if price cleanly breaks R1 or S1 with momentum.
Price inside CPR — indecision zone
Between TC and BC, neither side in control. Wait for a clean close outside CPR before any entry.
Low volatility first candle
First 9:15 candle under 80 pts Nifty / 150 pts BankNifty / 250 pts Sensex. Wait 2–3 candles after open for direction.
Supertrend conflicts with CPR
TC break with red Supertrend, or BC break with green. Two conflicting signals = no entry. Wait for full alignment.
Layer 2 — Institutional Bias

VWAP Scenario Simulator

VWAP is the volume-weighted average price from 9:15 AM, reset daily. Institutions benchmark against it. Price above VWAP = institutional buying. CPR gives the level — VWAP tells you whether institutions support it. Click each scenario.

Core rule: VWAP is reliable only after 9:30 AM. In the first 15 minutes it has too few data points. Before 9:30, trust CPR structure over VWAP.

Sizing with VWAP confirmation

All 3 aligned
CPR + VWAP + Supertrend
Size up: 2–3 lots. Maximum conviction.
Two aligned
CPR + Supertrend only
Normal size: 1 lot. One confirmation missing.
VWAP conflicts
The veto indicator
No trade when VWAP opposes CPR direction.
Expiry caveat: On Thursday (Nifty) / Wednesday (BankNifty) expiry, market makers pin VWAP near max pain. Don't read it as normal institutional bias on expiry days. Also: a CPR breakout that clears VWAP SD1 band is a strong trending signal — scale in rather than exiting at R1.

Divergence — exit signals

Bearish divergence — exit CE early
Price makes new high, RSI makes lower high. Book 50–75% at R1, trail rest tight. Read divergence on 15-min, not 5-min — far more reliable.
Bullish divergence — tighten PE stops
Price makes new low, RSI higher low. Selling momentum fading. Don't add shorts. Watch for CPR bounce forming above BC.
Divergence + R1 = full exit signal
CE position + price at R1 + bearish 15-min divergence — exit fully, don't trail. Rarely fails to produce a 30–50 pt reversal.

Layer 5 — Positional Intelligence

Volume Profile

Volume Profile is space-based, not time-based — it shows where volume actually traded at each price. Price moves fast through Low Volume Nodes (air pockets) and slows at High Volume Nodes. CPR gives the mathematical pivot; VP tells you if institutions are defending it.

The 80% Value Area Rule: If price opens outside yesterday's Value Area and re-enters it, there's ~80% probability it travels to the other side. Open above VAH → drops inside → expect VAL. One of the most underused edges in Nifty intraday — and a clean call-spread-selling setup on the re-entry.
POC aligns with TC — strongest long
Yesterday's most-traded price defending today's breakout level. Massive institutional backing. 2-lot setup when other layers align. Target VAH, then R1.
Open above VAH — trend day confirmed
Institutions bidding above fair value. Narrow CPR breakout above TC = high conviction. Target R1–R2. SL: close back below VAH.
Open below VAL — bearish trend day
Mirror setup. Narrow CPR breakdown below BC is high conviction. Target S1–S2. SL: close back above VAL.
Open inside Value Area — range day
Inside accepted fair value. CPR breakouts fail more — price is drawn back to POC. Wait for a clean Value Area exit before trusting any breakout.
LVN between TC and R1 — fast move
No institutional resistance in the path. Don't take partials inside the LVN — trail SL aggressively and let it run to R1.
HVN between TC and R1 — slow move
Institutional interest in the path. Take 50–60% profit at the HVN, trail the rest with SL at TC. Don't expect a clean first-attempt break.

Layer 6 — Options Market Intelligence

OI + PCR Console

The only layer that reads the options market itself. Writers — the smart money — defend their strikes, making max OI levels behave like invisible walls. When those walls align with CPR levels, conviction is highest.

What OI measures
Outstanding unsettled contracts per strike. High CE OI = writers want price below that strike. High PE OI = writers want price above it. They hedge and defend actively — real price friction.
OI increasing = walls building
CE OI rising while price rises = call writers reinforcing resistance (bearish). PE OI rising while price falls = put writers reinforcing support (bullish).
OI decreasing = walls coming down KEY SIGNAL
CE OI dropping as price approaches = short covering — expect a fast breakout, not rejection. The OI change column matters more than the OI column.
Max Pain — the gravity centre
The strike where total buyer premium loss is maximum. On expiry day Nifty gravitates to max pain by 3:00–3:15 PM. Note it every morning — it's often within 50–100 pts of the Pivot.

Pre-Market Routine

10–12 minutes before 9:15 AM. Doing this consistently eliminates reactive decision-making during the session.

1 · India VIX — 2 min
Check INDIAVIX level and direction vs yesterday. Moved 1.5+ pts overnight? Adjust strategy before anything else.
2 · CPR levels — 1 min
Write TC, BC, Pivot, R1, R2, S1, S2. Classify narrow vs wide. This sets the day's character before the open.
3 · Volume Profile — 2 min
Yesterday's POC, VAH, VAL. Any overlap with CPR within 20 pts = highest-conviction zones of the day.
4 · NSE option chain — 3 min
Max CE OI strike (ceiling), max PE OI strike (floor), max pain. Compare all three to CPR levels.
5 · PCR day bias — 2 min
Above 1.2 = bullish prior. Below 0.8 = bearish. 0.8–1.2 = wait for confirmation after the open.
6 · Three questions — 1 min
(1) CPR narrow or wide? (2) VP level overlapping CPR? (3) What does OI say about direction? These decide trade type and size before the first candle.

Expiry Day Rules

Nifty expires Thursday, BankNifty Wednesday. Expiry has a fundamentally different character — standard rules need modification.

VWAP is unreliable
Market makers pin VWAP near max pain through hedge adjustments. Don't read it as normal institutional bias.
Max pain overrides CPR from 2 PM
If price is far from max pain at 2 PM, expect a move toward it regardless of CPR signals.
Best expiry trade: ATM straddle sell near max pain after 2 PM
Final 90 minutes destroy remaining premium. Exit by 3:20 PM. Works whenever price is within ~100 pts of max pain at 2 PM.
No naked CE/PE into the last hour
Book or cut by 2:30 PM if price is far from max pain. The reversion will destroy unhedged positions on the wrong side.
0DTE morning: full system valid until 1 PM
From 1 PM, decay and max pain gravity dominate. Tighten SLs significantly. Exit most positions by 2:30 PM.
Timing summary: 9:30–11:00 standard system, normal size · 11:00–1:00 reduced size, tighter SLs · 1:00–2:00 high-conviction only · after 2:00 straddle/strangle selling near max pain only.
